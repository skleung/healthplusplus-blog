Incentives and Financial Returns

08 February 2015 on thinktank

This week we tackled the area of financial returns in investing in healthcare startups, Evan posed to us the question:

Are the financial returns for investing in a healthcare startup less than a technology startup? Why? If the perception is that they are less, how can that be changed?

In response, we tried to pinpoint various reasons why the barrier to entry in healthcare innovation is so high:

  • Higher risk in healthcare than technology ventures
  • Larger startup costs
  • Rules + Regulations
  • Depth of knowledge required

Evan Anderson, a 2004 graduate of the Stanford Biodesign program previously worked at Spiracur and the FDA. He currently works as a medical device consultant and is in the process of starting a new venture in dermatology.
